## Project:Examining Gender Pension Gaps: Trajectories Over Time in the UK

We are seeking a highly organised and motivated Research Assistant to support with a range of activities related to literature review, conceptual development, stakeholder analysis, impact activity planning, and project coordination. 

The Nuffield Foundation-funded project [Examining Gender Pension Gaps: Trajectories Over Time in the UK](https://www.nuffieldfoundation.org/project/examining-gender-pension-gaps-trajectories-over-time-in-the-uk) seeks to improve conceptual and empirical understanding of the UK’s Gender Pension Gap (GPeG). The project combines conceptual framework development with quantitative analysis of the Wealth and Assets Survey and related data sources. A key part of this first work package involves strengthening the conceptual, theoretical, and policy landscape around the GPeG, mapping stakeholders, and supporting dissemination and engagement.

### Main Duties and Responsibilities
Working under the direction of the Principal Investigator, the Research Assistant will:
1. **Literature Review and Conceptual Development**
- Conduct structured and systematic literature reviews on gender pension gaps, gendered financial inequalities, life-course approaches to pensions, and relevant policy frameworks.
- Produce well-structured annotated bibliographies and analytical summaries.
- Contribute to conceptual mapping exercises and the development of theoretical frameworks.
2. **Stakeholder Mapping and Engagement**
- Identify and map key stakeholders across policy, industry, academia, and civil society.
- Support stakeholder engagement planning, including maintaining contact lists and preparing briefing materials.
- Assist in stakeholder correspondence and meeting organisation.
3. **Dissemination and Communication**
- Draft accessible summaries of research for non-academic audiences, including policy briefs, blog posts, website copy, and social-media content.
- Contribute to visual or diagrammatic representations of concepts and findings.
- Support preparation of dissemination events and public-facing materials.
4. **Project Administration**
- Support coordination of team meetings, workshops, stakeholder interviews and events.
- Maintain project records, documentation, and shared digital organisation systems.
- Assist with ethics paperwork, GDPR compliance, and internal administrative requirements where necessary.
5. **Teamwork and Collaboration**
- Work closely with the PI at Birmingham and the Co-I at Manchester.
- Attend scheduled project meetings and provide regular progress updates.
- Contribute to an organised, inclusive, and collaborative research environment.
